● Mode collapse hypothesis is wrong. Both checkpoints have nearly identical
  action distributions — overall std is 0.340 for both, and the per-dimension
  variance looks the same. The policy is not becoming deterministic.

  What the diagnostic shows:
  - 50k and 100k output actions of the same magnitude and variance
  - Gripper (dim 7) is correctly fixed at 1.0 throughout
  - No evidence of "frozen" or repetitive outputs

  The drop from 50% → 10-20% isn't from variance collapse. More likely
  candidates:

  1. Transient training dip — cosine LR decay halves the learning rate at 100k.
  Some runs have a temporary success-rate dip that recovers by 150k-200k as the
  policy fine-tunes details. Very common with diffusion policies.
  2. Strategy shift — the policy at 50k learned a conservative "approach slowly"
   strategy that works. At 100k it may have learned a more aggressive strategy
  that overfits training trajectories but fails on slight variations.
  3. Sampling noise — 100 episodes at ~10% is only ~10 successes. That estimate
  is noisy.

  The most important thing is to check 150k when it lands. If it recovers toward
   50%+, it was just a dip. If it stays low, we have a real problem with the
  training configuration.
